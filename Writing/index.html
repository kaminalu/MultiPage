Projects

  • Note: Samples of print and digital design work currently available by request only.

  • Rendr Pop Up - Held May 2nd, 2018
    • A pop up art gallery featuring interactive technology at the 1871 Auditorium.
    • Project Timeline: May 2017 - May 2018 (1 year)
    • Role: Founder, Curator, Manager, Marketing
    • Responsibilities: Researched and developed the concept. Sourced designers, artists, sponsors, and vendors. Balanced very limited budget with needs of the event. Managed the timeline and tracked milestones. Used G Suite for organization and internal communication, Mailchimp for email marketing, Eventbrite and Facebook for ticket sales, Microsoft Suite for document generation and event graphics. Check out my Professional Event Portfolio.
  • 1871 International Women's Day Maker Fair - Held March 8th, 2018
    • Pop up shop featuring local female artisans & entrepreneurs. The Maker Fair was coordinated alongside the other events of International Women's Day in order to give opportunity and visibility to local female artisans and entrepreneurs.
    • Project Timeline: February 2018 - March 2018 (1 month)
    • Role: Project Coordinator
    • Responsibilities: Planned and executed vendor acquisition and relations for on-site event showcasing 30 local artisans. Primarily used G Suite for organization and communication.
  • Badass Books from the Women of 1871 - Compiled March 8th, 2018
    • A list of must-reads from 1871's female community. This project was created to strengthen and empower the fierce community of women entrepreneurs and professionals at 1871 through engagement and connection. It was originally built for International Women's Day 2018.
    • Project Timeline: March 7th, 2018 - March 8th, 2018 (1 day)
    • Role: Project Coordinator
    • Responsibilities: Launched the project by organizing community outreach, compiling contributions, and building the graphics and database. Used G Suite for communication, Canva for Marketing, and the Goodreads platform to compile the list.
  • A Search for Love in the White City - Published April 26th, 2018
    • A brief interactive, educational narrative that takes place during the 1893 World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago. Built using Twine during April, 2018, and hosted on itch.io.
    • Project Timeline: March 2018 - April 2018 (2 months)
    • Role: Project Manager, Narrative Designer
    • Responsibilities: Planned and executed the narrative premise including world-building, character design, interactive plot and dialog using the build parameters provided as part of the project. Used G Suite for communication, organization and collaboration, Lucidchart for decision tree mapping, Twine as the build platform and essentially "game engine," and the itch.io platform for publishing and hosting the game.
  • The Flaming Tomb of the Undying - A short, one-shot role-playing campaign based on Dungeon Squad 2. Written in February, 2018 and submitted into the Lewis College of Human Sciences Writing Contest.
  • Gem Harvest - A fifteen-minute eductional card game for 2-6 players ages 6 and up. Players try to stake their claim as the richest by collecting as many gemstones as they can to win. Designed for Dr. Carly Kocurek's Spring 2017 Game Design course.
  • Nineteen Eighty-Four - A pitch doc for an 8-bit graphic adventure game based on the classic novel written by George Orwell. Designed for Dr. Carly Kocurek's Spring 2017 Game Design course.

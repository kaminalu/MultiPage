Projects

  • Rendr Pop Up - Held May 6th, 2018 · A pop up art gallery featuring interactive technology held on May 2nd, 2018 at the 1871 Auditorium. Founded, curated, and managed by myself as part of my senior capstone project at Illinois Tech. Check out my Professional Event Portfolio.
  • 1871 International Women's Day Maker Fair - Held March 8th, 2018 · Pop up shop featuring local female artisans & entrepreneurs · The Maker Fair was coordinated alongside the other events of International Women's Day in order to give opportunity and visibility to local female artisans and entrepreneurs. · Project Coordinator, Google Suite · Planned and executed vendor acquisition and relations for on-site International Women's Day Maker Fair showcasing 30 local artisans.
  • Badass Books from the Women of 1871 - March 2018 · A list of must-reads from 1871's female community · Project Coordinator, Google Suite, Canva, Goodreads · This project was created to strengthen and empower the fierce community of women entrepreneurs and professionals at 1871 through engagement and connection. It was originally built for International Women's Day 2018. · Launched the project by organizing community outreach, compiling contributions, and building the graphics and database.
  • A Search for Love in the White City - A brief interactive narrative that takes place during the 1893 World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago. Built using Twine during April, 2018, and hosted on itch.io.
  • The Flaming Tomb of the Undying - A short, one-shot role-playing campaign based on Dungeon Squad 2. Written in February, 2018 and submitted into the Lewis College of Human Sciences Writing Contest.
  • Gem Harvest - A fifteen-minute card game for 2-6 players ages 6 and up. Players try to stake their claim as the richest by collecting as many gemstones as they can to win. Designed for Dr. Carly Kocurek's Spring 2017 Game Design course.
  • Nineteen Eighty-Four - A pitch doc for an 8-bit graphic adventure game based on the classic novel written by George Orwell. Designed for Dr. Carly Kocurek's Spring 2017 Game Design course.
